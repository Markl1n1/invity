Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Invity charge a fee for outgoing transactions?

  • The fee is applied to cover the network costs associated with transferring cryptocurrency to an external wallet or exchange.
  • It is important to keep in mind that on-chain fees may vary based on network activity. During periods of high congestion, fees may increase.
  • Outgoing bank transfers may incur fees due to the administrative and operational costs associated with processing and facilitating the secure transfer of funds between financial institutions. These fees contribute to maintaining the infrastructure and ensuring the efficiency and reliability of the transfer process.

I haven't received my account activation email. What should I do?

  • Please check that you have entered your e-mail address correctly and check your spam folder.
  • If you did not receive your activation e-mail within 30 minutes, please contact support@invity.co.uk or use webchat on this page to contact support specialist.

Do you collect my customers’ information?

  • We don’t collect any data on your customers except their email address (for refunds, notifications, etc.), which is never mandatory to provide. Our company strictly follows the guidelines of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
  • Learn more about it here

How is my account information protected?

  • We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect all personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, accidental loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure or access, particularly when the data processing involves the transmission of information over a network.
  • We also comply with the guidelines of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). You can find more information about data protection in our PrivacyPolicy

How can I make my account more secure?

  • We already take extensive security measures to ensure that your account is as safe as possible. However, there are some additional steps that we strongly recommend you take in order to protect your account from unauthorized access.
  • First off, keep in mind that Invity staff will never ask for your password, 2-factor authentication codes or any other login credentials. If anyone who claims to be associated with Invity Support requests such information, please contact us immediately. We will also never ask you to install a remote login or remote support software on your devices. Here’s what you can do to improve upon your security:
  • Use a complex and unique password. Preferably, the password shouldn’t be used anywhere else or shared with other people. It's also a good idea to change your password periodically (about every 3 months) and use a completely new password each time.
  • Use password managers. Credential managing is much easier when using secure, encrypted password storage such as 1Password or LastPass.

How can I change the email address associated with my Invity account?

  • Please contact our support team when you are done, and we will make the change for you. You can contact support via email support@invity.co.uk or webchat

Where does Invity get its exchange rates?

  • Invity takes advantage of multiple exchanges worldwide when calculating exchange rates for invoices. Our priority is always to ensure operational, regulatory, and liquidity requirements while striving to remain competitive in the market. To ensure that, Invity utilises proprietary algorithms that fetches real-time rates from exchange and adjusts it for risks associated with the transaction.
  • We also stay mindful of market volatility and offer a locked exchange rate for 20 minutes, giving a payee enough time to complete the payment. The exchange rate is openly disclosed on the payment invoice.
